🌍 How to Contribute to the Atlas of People and Place

Welcome! This guide takes you from the starting line (opening the Atlas) to the finish line (saving your contribution so it appears on the map).

1. Open the Atlas

Use this link: Open the Atlas. On mobile, it opens directly in your browser. The map centres on Scotland — zoom and pan to your area.

2. Find Your Place

Zoom in to the location you know, or use the search bar. You can also tap the locate me button to jump to your current location.

3. Start with a Photo 📸

Take a photo while standing at the site. On mobile, photos are geotagged automatically. In the popup, choose Add image to upload. The photo is stored in Wikimedia Commons and linked to the map.

4. Answer the Question ✍️

Each feature asks a short question (e.g. “What is this historic site?”). Type your answer, or choose from presets if available.

5. Tags Behind the Scenes 🏷️

Your answer automatically adds the correct OpenStreetMap tag (like historic=castle). You don’t need to know the syntax.

6. Save Your Edit 💾

Click Save. Your contribution is written into OpenStreetMap and will appear live in the Atlas within minutes.

7. Check and Share 🔗

Refresh to see your contribution. Share the Atlas link with friends or groups so they can add their knowledge too.

Tips

  • Be specific: “Battle of Bannockburn site” is more useful than “Historic site.”
  • Portrait photos display better on mobile.
  • Check the marker is in the right place.
  • Add links (Wikipedia, local history sites) if relevant.

Every photo, tag, and detail helps build a bilingual, open resource for everyone.
