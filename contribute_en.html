🌍 How to Contribute to the Atlas of People and Place

Welcome! This guide shows you how to add your knowledge and photos to the Atlas. Edits are saved automatically once you’re logged in — there is no separate “Save” button anymore.

1. Open the Atlas

Use this link: Open the Atlas. The map centres on Scotland — zoom and pan to your area.

2. Log in

Click Log in at the top right and sign in with your OpenStreetMap account. Without logging in, your edits won’t be saved.

3. Find Your Place

Zoom to the location you know, or use the search bar. You can also tap the locate me button to jump to your current location.

4. Add Details ✍️

Click on a point (or add a new one). A panel will open with questions. Type a name, description, or other details. As soon as you leave the field, your edit is uploaded automatically.

5. Add a Photo 📸

- Export your photo from your phone or computer.
- Upload it to Wikimedia Commons (you’ll need to be logged in there too).
- Copy the image link from Commons.
- Paste it into the “Add a photo” field in the Atlas.
- The photo will then appear in the map popup.

6. Check and Share 🔗

Refresh the map to see your contribution. Share the Atlas link with friends or groups so they can add their knowledge too.

Every photo, tag, and detail helps build a bilingual, open resource for everyone.
